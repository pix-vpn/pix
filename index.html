Can I be tracked using a VPN?

VPN gives you a new IP and encrypts the connection between your device and the internet, so no one can see the data you send and receive. However, there are other ways to track your online actions. Hackers may install malicious software (keyloggers, spyware, etc.) on your device. Or, if you share a lot of information online, and each account uses the same username, someone good at Googling can gather a lot of information about you. A VPN is not an all-in-one solution for protecting your internet traffic, but an extra layer of security.

Is it legal to use a VPN?

VPNs are legal in many countries, but some countries ban VPNs entirely or strictly regulate them. In such countries, using a VPN is expected to circumvent government censorship and gain internet access. However, if caught, you could face hefty fines. In any case, if you decide to do this, be sure to download the app and subscribe before you travel.

Are there alternatives to VPNs?

You can use other software that functions similarly to a VPN, but there is no substitute for the comprehensive security and privacy a VPN provides. Smart DNS can help you access blocked websites and online services, but it doesn't encrypt your traffic. Using Tor (The Onion Routing) protects privacy, but many websites are inaccessible. Therefore, if you want a service that protects your privacy while providing a new IP address while encrypting your data, we recommend choosing a reliable VPN provider.

Is it dangerous to use a VPN?

If you choose a trusted VPN service provider, you are absolutely safe. However, don't be tempted by free VPNs. Free VPNs will track your online activity, the websites you visit, your interactions, and sell to the highest bidder. Free VPNs don't properly encrypt your connection; they're also prone to IP leaks and DNS leaks, and may even contain malware or spyware throughout your local area network. When it comes to free VPNs, there are dangers in using them, so do your research to choose the best VPN provider.

Should I keep the VPN on all the time?

Yes, with a few exceptions, you should always have the VPN turned on. It's the only way to ensure your true location is hidden, your online data is encrypted, and your browsing privacy is protected.

What should you look out for when choosing a VPN?

How fast is it? Will it keep my data safe? Will it respect my privacy?
